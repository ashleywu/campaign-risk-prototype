## Tools

Any AI tools allowed and encouraged (Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, Replit, Notion, etc.)

## The problem

HTS Media runs sponsored campaigns across travel surfaces. A recurring challenge is identifying campaigns at risk of under-delivery early enough to intervene.

In practice:

- Campaign data is incomplete or messy
- Signals come from multiple places (budget, timing, performance, notes)
- Teams need a clear recommendation, not raw metrics

## Your task

Build a very lightweight prototype that demonstrates how an internal user could:

1. View a set of active campaigns  
2. Understand which ones are at risk and why  
3. See a suggested next action for each campaign

You should:

- Create your own small test dataset (10–20 campaigns is enough)
- Connect that dataset to the front end you build
- Use AI where helpful to generate logic, insights, or recommendations

The goal is not polish — it’s clarity and usefulness.

## What to build

At minimum, your prototype should include:

- A simple front end (table, cards, dashboard, etc.)
- Campaign-level inputs such as:
  - Budget
  - Spend to date
  - Start / end date
  - Status or notes (e.g. “launch delayed”, “waiting on assets”)
- An AI-assisted output that:
  - Flags risk (or prioritizes campaigns)
  - Explains why in plain language
  - Suggests one concrete action per campaign

## Deliverable

Please submit:

- One link or artifact (Notion, Figma, Replit, simple web app, etc.)
- 5–7 bullets covering:
  - What the prototype does
  - Who it’s for
  - What assumptions you made
  - What you’d improve next if this were real

Optional (not required): a short video walkthrough.

## What we’re evaluating

We are not evaluating:

- UI polish
- Perfect data modeling
- Production-ready code

We are evaluating:

- How you structure an ambiguous problem
- How you use AI as a tool, not a crutch
- Whether the output supports real decisions
- Judgment around scope and tradeoffs

## Notes

- There is no single “right” solution
